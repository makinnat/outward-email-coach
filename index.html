The Approach

What makes an outward email different?

An outward mindset means seeing the people you email as real human beings with their own needs, challenges, and objectives — not as targets on a list. When your email reflects that, everything changes: open rates go up, replies feel genuine, and conversations start from a place of trust.

🚫

Inward emails sound like this

"We offer a cutting-edge platform that helps companies leverage synergies..." — It's all about you, your product, and what you want to sell. The reader is a prop in your story.

✅

Outward emails sound like this

"I noticed your team is growing fast across 12 locations — that kind of expansion usually surfaces leadership challenges that are hard to solve with training alone..." — It's about them.

🚫

Inward emails close like this

"Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call?" — Hedging, apologetic, and making the reader do all the work. You don't sound like you believe in what you're offering.

✅

Outward emails close like this

"What's the outcome your leadership team needs to deliver this year? Let's schedule 15 minutes next week to talk through it." — Direct, confident, and centered on their world.

How It Works

Two ways to use this tool

Whether you're writing from scratch or reviewing a finished draft, the coach has you covered.

1

Write with live coaching

Use the Write tab to compose your email with real-time tips as you type. Fill in who you're writing to, what outcome you want, and what their job to be done is — the coach adapts its suggestions to your specific situation.

2

Grade a finished email

Switch to the Grade tab and paste any email to get an instant score (A through F) with detailed feedback on what's working, what's not, and exactly how to fix it. Each issue includes before/after examples specific to your email.

3

Coach through the fixes

After grading, use the coaching chat to work through issues interactively. Say "help me fix the opener" or "help me sound more like myself" and the coach walks you through it step by step.
